## BACKGROUND

- **October 2025:** Initial Full and Individual Evaluation (FIE) was completed. The district identified only Specific Learning Disability in Reading Fluency, Emotional Disturbance, and Other Health Impairment (ADHD).

- **November/December 2025:** We raised concerns that the evaluation failed to identify dyslexia, written expression difficulties, and dysgraphia. We requested the district expand the eligibility findings and corresponding services. **The district refused.**

- **April 2026:** A follow-up evaluation (conducted by Elaine Crawley) now confirms what we advocated for in November/December — Paxton meets eligibility for:
  - Specific Learning Disability in Reading Fluency
  - **Dyslexia**
  - **Specific Learning Disability in Written Expression**
  - **Dysgraphia**

This represents a significant expansion of the original eligibility findings.

---

## THE HARM

For approximately **6 months (November 2025 – April 2026)**, Paxton did not receive appropriate interventions for dyslexia, written expression, and dysgraphia because the district refused to recognize these disability areas in the initial evaluation.

During this critical period:
- Paxton's written expression deficits went unaddressed
- Dysgraphia-specific interventions were not provided
- Writing goals and services were not included in the IEP
- Interventions that could have prevented regression were not implemented

**Our child was denied services he was entitled to because the district's evaluation was incomplete.**

## COMPENSATORY SERVICES REQUESTED

We respectfully request the following compensatory services be documented in Paxton's IEP:

### 1. Extended Dyslexia Services
- **Rationale:** Paxton was eligible for dyslexia services earlier but was not properly identified until April 2026.
- **Request:** Additional 30-60 hours of one-on-one Orton-Gillingham based dyslexia instruction beyond current service minutes.

### 2. Written Expression Services
- **Rationale:** SLD-Written Expression was not identified until April 2026.
- **Request:** Direct specialized instruction in written expression, including:
  - Sentence construction
  - Paragraph development
  - Composition skills
  - Spelling/orthographic skills

### 3. Occupational Therapy for Dysgraphia
- **Rationale:** Dysgraphia was not identified until April 2026. Parent-reported early fine motor concerns.
- **Request:** OT evaluation and direct services targeting:
  - Handwriting fluency
  - Letter formation
  - Fine motor coordination for written work

### 4. Extended School Year (ESY)
- **Rationale:** To prevent regression of newly identified skill deficits and provide intensive intervention.
- **Request:** ESY services for Summer 2026 focusing on reading, writing, and fine motor skills.

### 5. Progress Monitoring and Documentation
- **Request:** Quarterly progress reports on all compensatory goals, with data shared with parents within 5 business days of each reporting period.

## LEGAL BASIS

Under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), schools are required to provide a Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) that includes special education and related services designed to meet the unique needs of students with disabilities. 20 U.S.C. § 1401(9).

When a district fails to properly identify a student's disability needs, resulting in a denial of appropriate services, compensatory services are an appropriate remedy to make the student whole. Texas Education Code and federal regulations support this position.

The district had an obligation to conduct a comprehensive evaluation that considered all areas of suspected disability. The October 2025 evaluation was incomplete, and the district's refusal to address our concerns in November/December resulted in a denial of FAPE.
