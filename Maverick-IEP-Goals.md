# IEP Goal Proposals for Maverick

*Prepared for IEP Team Review*

---

## Current Performance Summary

Maverick is a student with Autism, ADHD, and Speech impairment who demonstrates:

- **Significant frustration tolerance deficits** — will quit tasks after 2-3 sentences, rips up work when frustrated
- **Fine motor fatigue** — writing endurance limited; avoids cursive, detailed cutting, buttoning
- **Pre-meltdown escalation** — exhibits "giddy/manic" state (hyperactivity, inappropriate laughter) prior to meltdowns or elopement attempts
- **Transition difficulties** — struggles to transition away from preferred activities, especially screens
- **Fatigue-amplified dysregulation** — behaviors escalate significantly when overtired; bedtime routine is challenging

---

## Proposed IEP Goals

### Goal 1: Self-Regulation & Early Identification

**Given** visual or verbal cueing from staff, **Maverick will** identify and communicate early signs of frustration or dysregulation (verbally or via signal) **and** request a break or implement a regulation strategy **in 4 out of 5 opportunities** across 3 consecutive data days.

*Rationale:* Currently, Maverick does not consistently recognize his own escalation window. Teaching him to identify early signs empowers him to access supports before full dysregulation.

---

### Goal 2: Communication-Replacement for Elopement

**When** Maverick feels the urge to leave a setting, **he will** communicate his need to a staff member (verbally, via picture card, or signal) **prior to leaving** the designated area, **in 80% of opportunities** across 4 consecutive data points.

*Rationale:* Elopement appears tied to dysregulation and sensory overwhelm. Teaching a functional communication alternative reduces safety incidents and replaces escape behavior with a socially appropriate tool.

---

### Goal 3: Frustration Tolerance for Written Expression

**Given** a writing task at his instructional level, **Maverick will** independently complete at least 4 sentences (or 2+ minutes of sustained fine motor output) **with** fewer than 2 verbal protests and without requesting task abandonment, **in 4 out of 5 trials** across 3 consecutive data days.

*Rationale:* Current tolerance is 2-3 sentences before refusal. Building incrementally to 4+ sentences increases academic participation and reduces avoidance behaviors.

---

### Goal 4: Transition from Preferred Activities

**Following** a 5-minute warning (visual timer), **Maverick will** transition away from preferred activities **within 2 minutes** with no more than 1 verbal protest, **across 4 consecutive data points**.

*Rationale:* Transitions are a known challenge. With the 5-minute warning system already in place at home, generalizing this structure to school will increase compliance and reduce escalation.

---

### Goal 5: Regulation During Pre-Meltdown State

**When** Maverick displays pre-meltdown indicators (excessive motor activity, inappropriate laughter, "I can't stop!" statements), **staff will** implement a de-escalation protocol (offer sensory support, reduce demands, provide safe exit), **resulting in** Maverick returning to baseline regulation **within 5 minutes in 4 out of 5 incidents**.

*Rationale:* This goal establishes a team-wide response protocol for the specific "giddy/manic" state. It ensures consistent, proactive intervention rather than reactive responses.

---

## Recommended Services

- **Speech-Language Therapy:** Continue for pragmatic language and self-advocacy skills
- **Occupational Therapy:** Add or continue for fine motor endurance, sensory regulation
- **Behavior Support:** Consultation for FBA update and BIP revision given elopement patterns
- **Counseling/Social Work:** Emotional regulation strategies, frustration tolerance curriculum

---

## Proposed Accommodations

1. **Reduced writing length demands** — alternate assessment methods (verbal responses, speech-to-text)
2. **Visual transition timers** — for all preferred activities
3. **Sensory regulation toolkit** — access to quiet space, weighted lap pad, heavy work activities
4. **Break card/system** — allow self-initiated breaks without penalty
5. **Early warning system** — staff trained to recognize pre-meltdown indicators
6. **Fatigue-aware scheduling** — avoid demanding tasks during late afternoon; consider adjusted day if needed
