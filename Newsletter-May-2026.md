## The End of School Year Is Here — Is Your Child's IEP Gathering Dust?

If you're like most parents, you signed an IEP last fall feeling hopeful. Now here we are, May, and you're wondering: *Is this actually helping my child?*

Here's the honest truth — many IEPs aren't working not because schools don't care, but because **the plan wasn't built for your child's actual needs**.

---

## 5 Reasons Your IEP Might Be Falling Short

### 1. The Goals Are Vague
"Student will improve reading skills" isn't a goal — it's a hope. IEP goals need to be specific, measurable, and time-bound. If you can't track it, you can't measure progress.

### 2. Services Don't Match the Needs
Your child might qualify for speech therapy, but what if the real barrier is *how* they're accessing the curriculum? Services should address the root cause, not just the label.

### 3. Progress Isn't Being Measured
If you're not seeing quarterly progress reports that show actual data, something's missing. Schools are required to track goal progress — if they aren't, that's a red flag.

### 4. The IEP Was Built Around Availability, Not Your Child
Sometimes services are limited by what the district *can* offer, not what your child *needs*. An IEP should be individualized — not a menu of what's convenient.

### 5. Nobody Told You About Compensatory Services
If your child didn't receive the services outlined in the IEP, they may be owed **compensatory services** — extra help to make up for what was missed. This is a big deal, and many parents don't know it exists.

---

## What You Can Do Now

**🔍 Request a copy of all progress monitoring data** — Ask for the data sheets, graphs, and reports that show whether your child is meeting IEP goals.

**📅 Request an IEP review meeting** — You don't have to wait for the annual review. If things aren't working, you can call a meeting anytime.

**📝 Document everything** — Keep records of what was promised vs. what was delivered. Dates, emails, conversations.

**🤝 Bring an advocate** — If meetings have felt one-sided, you're allowed to bring someone with you. That's what I'm here for.
