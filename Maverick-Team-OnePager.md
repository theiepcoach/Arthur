# Understanding Maverick: A Behavior Support Guide

*For school staff and IEP team members*

---

## What You're Seeing Is Not What It Seems

When Maverick becomes giddy, silly, or "manic" before a meltdown or elopement, this is **not** excitement, defiance, or a game. It's **nervous system dysregulation** — his brain is in a state of hyperarousal and trying to release pressure before the storm.

**This is a neurological event, not a behavioral choice.**

---

## The Escalation Cycle

1. **Accumulating pressure** → Demands exceed his capacity
2. **Pre-meltdown state (what you're seeing now)** → Hyperactivity, inappropriate laughter, running, "I can't stop!"
3. **Meltdown OR shutdown** → Full dysregulation or complete withdrawal
4. **Recovery** → Needs quiet, low-demand time

**The earlier we catch #2, the better we can de-escalate.**

---

## Observable Signs to Watch For

- ↑ motor activity (pacing, bouncing, running)
- Inappropriate laughter or silliness that escalates
- Verbal statements: "I can't stop!", "I don't want to!"
- Physical cues: flushed skin, dilated pupils, rigid body
- Flattened affect immediately after (shut down)

---

## What Works (and What Doesn't)

### ✅ What Works
- **Offer sensory outlets** — heavy work (pushing, pulling), deep pressure, quiet space
- **Narrate without demanding**: "I see you're feeling big right now. Let's get some air."
- **Reduce demands** — don't try to talk him down or reason
- **Have a safe exit plan** — a place he can go without judgment

### ❌ What Doesn't Work
- Reasoning with him during dysregulation
- Threats or consequences mid-escalation
- Adding demands ("finish this first")
- Physical restraint unless safety requires it
- Making him "earn" re-entry

---

## How to Talk About It

| Instead of saying... | Say... |
|---------------------|--------|
| "He's being manipulative" | "His nervous system is in survival mode — fight, flight, or freeze" |
| "He just wants attention" | "He's seeking escape from overwhelming demands" |
| "He can control it if he tries" | "The prefrontal cortex goes offline during dysregulation" |
| "He needs to learn consequences" | "Consequences don't teach during shutdown — they increase shame" |

---

## Key Message

> **Maverick is not choosing this.** His brain is responding to overwhelming input. Our job is to support his nervous system back to regulation — not to punish him for a neurological event he cannot control in the moment.
